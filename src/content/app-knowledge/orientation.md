# Orientation

## What Is Orientation?

Orientation is the first thing new students complete when they join the SPOKES program. It walks you through all the required forms and documents so you can get started with classes, certifications, and goal-setting.

## Required Forms

During orientation you will read and sign several documents, including:

- Student Profile
- Personal Attendance Contract
- Rights and Responsibilities
- Dress Code Policy
- Authorization for Release of Information
- DoHS Release of Information
- Media Release Form
- Technology Acceptable Use Policy
- Employment Portfolio Checklist
- Learning Needs Screening Instrument
- CTE Learning Styles Assessment

Your teacher may add or adjust items depending on program requirements for the current year.

## How to Complete Orientation

Orientation appears as a checklist in VisionQuest. Each item opens the document for you to review and sign digitally. Work through the list in order — check off each item as you finish it. Some forms are fillable PDFs where you type in your information, and some just need your acknowledgment.

## Orientation Checklist

The checklist shows your progress at a glance. Items are grouped into sections, and you can see which ones are complete, which are in progress, and which still need attention. Your goal is to finish everything in one session if possible.

## What Happens After Orientation

Once you complete all required orientation items, the Orientation link disappears from your sidebar and you gain access to the full set of VisionQuest features. Your Home dashboard will start showing personalized next actions like setting your first goal or beginning coursework.

## For Teachers

Teachers manage orientation templates through Program Setup. You can see which students have incomplete orientation, track individual form status, and follow up with students who need to finish. The intervention queue flags students with stalled orientation.
