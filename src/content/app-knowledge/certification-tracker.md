# Certification Tracker

## Available Certifications

SPOKES students can earn a variety of industry-recognized certifications, including:

- **IC3 Digital Literacy** — Three levels covering computing fundamentals, key applications, and living online. Passing all three earns the Master Certification.
- **Microsoft Office Specialist (MOS)** — Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Access certifications demonstrating Office proficiency.
- **ACT WorkKeys (NCRC)** — National Career Readiness Certificate with Bronze, Silver, Gold, or Platinum levels based on Applied Math, Workplace Documents, and Business Writing scores.
- **Intuit Certifications** — QuickBooks Certified User, Bookkeeping Professional, and more.
- **Customer Service** — "Through the Customer's Eyes" Parts 1 and 2.
- **Bring Your A Game** — Work ethic certification covering the 7 A's (Attitude, Attendance, Appearance, Ambition, Accountability, Appreciation, Acceptance).
- **Computer Essentials** and **Work Essentials** — Digital literacy and workplace readiness programs.
- **Critical Career Skills** — Generative AI Foundations and Professional Communications.

## Tracking Your Progress

The Learning page shows which certifications you have started, which you are working on, and which you have completed. You can see status, dates, and any evidence attached to each certification.

## How Certifications Connect to Ready to Work

The Ready to Work Certificate is the standard goal for all SPOKES students. Earning core certifications is one of the key requirements. Your certification progress feeds directly into your readiness score, so completing certifications moves you closer to program completion.

## Evidence and Proof

When you pass a certification exam, you can upload your score report or certificate to VisionQuest. This evidence is visible to your teacher and can also appear in your portfolio for employers.

## For Teachers

Teachers can view each student's certification progress from the student detail view. The system flags students who are behind on expected certifications, and unfinished certifications show up in the intervention queue when action is needed.
