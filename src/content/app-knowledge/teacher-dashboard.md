# Teacher Dashboard

## Class Overview

The Students page is your primary workspace. It opens with a class overview showing how your students are doing at a glance — enrollment counts, completion rates, and overall class health.

## Intervention Queue

Above the class overview sits the intervention queue. This is an urgency-scored list of students who need your attention right now. The queue surfaces three types of issues:

- **Alerts** — Specific problems like missing forms, overdue requirements, or flagged evidence gaps.
- **Inactivity** — Students who have not been active recently, scored by how long they have been quiet.
- **Goal Reviews** — Goals that are stale or need teacher confirmation.

Each item shows the student name, the reason they need attention, severity, and a quick action you can take. The goal is for you to identify and act on your top priorities in under five minutes.

## Student Detail View

Clicking on a student opens their detail view, organized into four tabs:

- **Overview** — Identity information, readiness score, top alerts, career discovery summary, and mood trends. Also where you handle password resets, deactivation, and archiving.
- **Goals & Plan** — The student's full goal tree (BHAG through tasks), pathway plan, evidence, and review queue. This is where you confirm, restate, or adjust goals.
- **Progress** — Orientation status, certification progress, portfolio contents, and conversation history. A comprehensive view of what the student has accomplished.
- **Operations** — Forms, notes, appointments, tasks, and the student's SPOKES record. Administrative actions live here.

## Finding Students Who Need Help

The intervention queue does the finding for you. Students are ranked by urgency so the most pressing issues appear first. You can also search for specific students by name or filter by class. If a student is not in the queue, they are generally on track.
